Artificial intelligence promises transformative business outcomes — but without the right data foundation, AI projects often fail to deliver. Here's how to build a strategy that works.

## The Data-First Imperative

Before any AI initiative can succeed, organisations need high-quality, accessible data. The most sophisticated machine learning model is only as good as the data it's trained on.

Common data challenges we encounter:

- **Data silos** — Critical information locked in disconnected systems
- **Data quality** — Inconsistent, incomplete, or outdated records
- **Governance gaps** — No clear ownership or lifecycle management
- **Infrastructure constraints** — On-premises systems that limit scalability

## Building Your Data Platform

A modern data platform typically consists of:

1. **Data ingestion layer** — ETL/ELT pipelines that reliably move data from source systems
2. **Storage and processing** — Cloud data warehouses (Snowflake, BigQuery, Databricks)
3. **Data governance** — Catalogues, lineage tracking, and access controls
4. **Analytics and AI layer** — BI tools, feature stores, and model serving infrastructure

## From Data to AI

Once the data foundation is solid, AI initiatives have a much higher chance of success. Practical applications Skillfield has delivered include:

- **Predictive maintenance** for manufacturing and utilities clients
- **Customer churn prediction** for subscription-based businesses
- **Automated anomaly detection** for financial services
- **Natural language processing** for document processing and compliance

## Measuring Success

AI and data projects need clear metrics from the start. We help clients define:

- **Business KPIs** — Revenue impact, cost reduction, efficiency gains
- **Technical metrics** — Model accuracy, latency, data freshness
- **Operational metrics** — Pipeline reliability, data quality scores
