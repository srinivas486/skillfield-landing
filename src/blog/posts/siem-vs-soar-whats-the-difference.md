Security teams are often presented with both SIEM and SOAR solutions. While these technologies complement each other, they serve fundamentally different functions.

## What Is SIEM?

**Security Information and Event Management (SIEM)** is a platform that collects, aggregates, and analyses security event data from across your entire IT environment in real time.

Key SIEM capabilities:

- **Log collection** — Aggregating logs from servers, endpoints, firewalls, and applications
- **Correlation** — Identifying patterns and relationships across disparate events
- **Alerting** — Notifying security teams of suspicious activity
- **Compliance reporting** — Generating audit trails for regulatory requirements (PCI DSS, ISO 27001, etc.)
- **Forensic investigation** — Historical data search for incident analysis

## What Is SOAR?

**Security Orchestration, Automation and Response (SOAR)** takes things a step further by automating the response to security incidents that SIEM (or other tools) detect.

Key SOAR capabilities:

- **Playbook automation** — Codifying response procedures as automated workflows
- **Tool integration** — Connecting your entire security stack (EDR, firewall, ticketing)
- **Case management** — Tracking incidents from detection through resolution
- **Metrics and reporting** — Measuring SOC performance and mean time to respond (MTTR)

## The Key Difference

Think of it this way:

- **SIEM = Detection and visibility** — "Something suspicious is happening"
- **SOAR = Response and automation** — "Here's what we're doing about it automatically"

A SIEM tells your team there's a problem. A SOAR helps your team — or automates — the response.

## Do You Need Both?

For most organisations with a dedicated security operations function, **yes** — both add value and work better together.

The decision depends on:

- **Team size** — Smaller teams benefit more from SOAR automation
- **Alert volume** — High-volume environments need automation to scale
- **Maturity level** — Start with SIEM visibility, then add SOAR automation as processes mature
